Frequently Asked Questions about OMG (Oh My Genes)



Q: Does OMG accpet Excel file format, for example, xlxs?
A: No. It accepts only plain text files, with .txt or .xls as file name extensions.
The file must contain three columns, gene id, control sample, and treatment sample.

Q: How long do you keep my uploaded file?
A: We don't keep your file. Your uploaded file will be deleted immediately after showing results.

Q:What is the use of the progress bar at the bottom of the chart?
A:You can use the mouse to drag the progress bar or the scroll wheel to control the size of the progress bar to see different ranges of F and C of points.

Q:What does the progress bar at the bottom left of the chart do?
A:You can use the mouse to drag the small triangle to show different ranges of log2(F/C) of points.

Q: Where can I find my differentially expressed genes?
A: The differentially expressed genes are represented as red dots (for up-regulation) above the highest red line or as blue dots
below the lowest red line. They can also be found in the table below the scatter plot, where most up-regulated genes are on
the top and most down-regulated genes are on the bottom.
